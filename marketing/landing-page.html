Why it exists

Starting is messy, even when building is easier.

AI made it easier than ever to make something. But easier does not always mean better.

The new risk is fake progress.

Pretty screens, generic features, and a weekend build can feel productive while still solving no real problem for no real customer.

The beginning needs sharper thinking.

Maker Compass slows the first step down just enough to make the work clearer: what it is, who it is for, and what should be built first.

The output should be usable.

You leave with structured research, product direction, first version scope, mockup paths, and technical next steps.

Less

Process

You bring the idea. Maker Compass finds the first step.

01

Capture the rough idea

Start with the thing in your head, even if it is messy, incomplete, or too broad.

02

Pressure-test the space

Understand the market, the alternatives, and the reasons people might care.

03

Choose the first version

Define the smallest serious version, the must-have flow, and the cuts that keep it focused.

04

Hand off the work

Use the plan, mockups, and technical direction to build with a clearer prompt and a better target.
